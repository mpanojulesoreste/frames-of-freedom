Frames of Freedom

Exploring resistance through art in the Caribbean from the late 20th century to the present

Course Overview

This course explores the role of different art forms in Caribbean Resistance movements from the late 20th century to the 21st century. Through music, film, photography and literary works, we will examine how Caribbean artists documented, challenged and reimagined colonial legacies, social inequalities and social erasures.

We will pay special attention to art acting as a catalyst for political consciousness, collective movements, anti-colonial movements and contemporary fights against globalization and displacement. Each week a different medium will be focused onâ€” music, photography and film, and literary work that captures the unique ways in which Caribbean people actively combat Western visions of civilization.

Through primary and secondary work, students will gain a deeper understanding of how Caribbean artists frame freedom, resist oppression and even envision a brighter future!

This course will also work to illustrate the ways in which mobilization efforts brought about liberation in unique ways. The course content will present different imaginations of freedom and how these various forms of freedom echo throughout time.
