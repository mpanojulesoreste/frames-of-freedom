Course Policies

Important guidelines, grading criteria, and academic integrity policies for this course.

Grading Breakdown

30%
Weekly Writing Assignments
Critical engagement with primary sources
40%
Final Project or Essay
Creative project or comparative analysis

Students are highly encouraged to attend all lectures to not only get the most of the course, but to also prepare for how to tackle weekly writing assignments and the final. While attendance is not recorded in lecture, students will be graded on their participation in lecture. Participation goes beyond showing up to class. Students are to critically engage with course materials and lecture presentations conducted by the instructor. Think of this course as a non-traditional lecture.

Successful weekly writing assignments go beyond summarizing texts. Students should practice close-reading of primary sources and asking necessary questions surrounding the historical time period in which these texts were written, why they were written, how they were written, and the arguments that are being proposed.

The final assignment is a major portion of your grade. While students can choose between the creative project or the comparative essay, students are highly encouraged to begin thinking about how they will approach the assignment two weeks before the due date. Please meet with course instructors in office hours with an outline before submission.

Note: Attendance of Caribbean public events on campus during the semester can earn you extra credit.

Academic Integrity

AI Usage Policy

As previously mentioned, this course draws on the intellectual and artistic work of authors who have been interrogating and exploring visions of freedom in the Caribbean as well as modes of resistance. I trust that students will rely on their abilities to intellectually and critically engage with these texts. While the use of AI generated software is not entirely prohibited in this course, students are not allowed to use such software for weekly writing assignments and the final.

If students need more clarity on texts, resources like office hours and writing resources on campus are highly encouraged. AI generated software such as ChatGPT, and Microsoft Copilot should only be used for brief summaries and/or historical context(s). Other AI generated software such as NotebookLM is strictly prohibited.

Students who are found to have used this software will be penalized by the Honor Code Committee. Students who have used permissible AI generated software as stated above for writing assignments, and more specifically, for forming thesis arguments, will also be penalized by the Honor Code Committee.

Grading Rubric

Paper Grading Criteria

Grade Criteria
A An "A" paper demonstrates clear and effective writing, as well as the cultivation of a strong thesis statement. Students who receive such a grade also demonstrate originality and well conducted research on the topic at hand.
B A "B" paper meets the requirements of what constitutes a good paper, but may be lacking in strong organization and/or clear development of ideas.
C A "C" paper aims to satisfy the requirements that lead to a good argument, but fails to demonstrate clear understanding of the material. Students who receive such a grade also fail to effectively construct an argument.
D A "D" paper barely meets the requirements that make up a decently fair paper. Students who receive such a grade do not demonstrate any engagement with course materials. There's no clear structure in their work and an argument is not identifiable.
F An "F" paper is a failed assignment. None of the requirements were met by the student.

Creative Project Grading Criteria

Grade Criteria
A An "A" creative project demonstrates originality, thoughtful engagement with course themes, and a strong conceptual vision of freedom in the Caribbean. The project clearly connects artistic choices to historical and political contexts and shows a sophisticated understanding of how Caribbean artists have resisted colonial and global structures. The accompanying artist's statement is well-written, articulates the project's intellectual and creative intentions, and effectively integrates primary and secondary sources. All citations follow Chicago style, and a bibliography is included.
B A "B" project meets the requirements of what constitutes a good project, but may be lacking in strong organization and/or clear development of ideas.
C A "C" project meets basic expectations but may lack a compelling or coherent vision. The creative component shows minimal engagement with the themes of the course or appears underdeveloped. The artist's statement is vague or superficial, offering little analysis or unclear connections to course concepts and assigned materials. Sources are present but may be misused or inconsistently cited. The project lacks depth in either form or content.
D A "D" project barely meets the requirements that make up a decently fair paper. Students who receive such a grade demonstrate limited effort and minimal engagement with course themes and materials. The creative work may be incomplete, disorganized, or unrelated to the assignment's objectives. The artist's statement may be missing, too brief, or lacking any substantive connection to course sources or ideas. Few or no citations are included, and Chicago style is not followed.
F An "F" project is a failed assignment. None of the requirements were met by the student.
